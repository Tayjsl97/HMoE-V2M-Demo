Abstract

Video-to-Music Generation (V2M) aims to automatically synthesize background music conditioned on video content. However, existing methods typically adopt a uniform feature extraction and fusion strategy for specified video type, failing to account for the inherently heterogeneous nature of diverse video content and thus limiting their adaptability across different video types. To address this limitation, we propose HMoE-V2M, a DiT-based universal video-to-music generation framework that introduces a hierarchical Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) mechanism across multiple levels of the generation pipeline, enabling a single model to adaptively serve diverse video types. Specifically, HMoE-V2M comprises three hierarchically connected levels of MoE: (1) a Feature Fusion MoE (FF-MoE) that dynamically routes over a constructed feature bank comprising multi-granularity semantic features and multi-type rhythmic features, and learns to adaptively weight them via a gating network conditioned on extracted features and diffusion timestep, producing a content- and timestep-aware fused condition for subsequent cross-modal alignment. (2) a Cross-attention Mixture-of-Heads (CA-MoH) in which two sequential cross-attention layers attend to semantic and rhythmic fused features respectively, with attention heads within each layer differentially activated to model fine-grained cross-modal alignment. (3) a denoising Feed-Forward Network MoE (FFN-MoE) that adaptively selects the most suitable FFN expert conditioned on the diffusion timestep, completing the end-to-end adaptive generation pipeline. Extensive experiments conducted across seven datasets, including six datasets spanning silent instrument performance, dance, and music video scenarios, as well as a held-out general-purpose benchmark, demonstrate that HMoE-V2M consistently outperforms prior methods across most video types, as evidenced by both objective metrics and subjective evaluations. Further routing behavior analysis reveals meaningful differentiation in how each level of MoE responds to different video types and diffusion timesteps.
